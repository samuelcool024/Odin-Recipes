Igbo Cuisine

Igbo cuisine features a variety of flavorful dishes, from soups to porridges and salads.
Popular options include Ofe Onugbu (Bitterleaf Soup), Ofe Nsala (White Soup), Ukwa (African Breadfruit),
Abacha (African Salad), and Nkwobi (Spicy Cow Leg).
Here are recipes for two common and highly regarded Igbo dishes:

Abacha (African Salad)

Abacha is a popular and flavourful snack or light meal made from shredded cassava.

Ingredients

  • 600g dry abacha (shredded cassava)
  • 2 cups ugba/ukpaka (sliced oil bean)
  • 1 cup palm oil
  • 2 tbsps powdered potash (akanwu)
  • Assorted meat, fish, or ponmo (cow skin), cooked and sliced (optional)
  • 1 large onion, chopped (plus extra for garnish)
  • 6 garden eggs, diced (optional)
  • Garden egg leaves/utazi leaves, chopped
  • Salt and dry pepper to taste
  • 4 tbsps ground crayfish
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Ogiri (castor bean paste), optional
Instructions
  1. Soak the Abacha: Soak the dry abacha in cold water for about 10-15 minutes until it softens. ,
    Drain and rinse with warm water, then set aside in a sieve.
  2. Prepare the Oil Mixture: In a pot or bowl, dissolve the potash in a little water and sieve the liquid.
    Stir the potash water with the palm oil until it thickens and changes to a yellowish paste (locally called "ncha").
  3. Mix Spices: Place the pot on low heat (or off the heat if preferred). Add the ground ehu, pepper, crayfish,
    seasoning cubes, ogiri (if using), and chopped onions to the oil mixture. Stir well to combine the flavors.
  4. Combine Ingredients: Add the softened abacha and ugba to the pot. Mix thoroughly until all ingredients
    are evenly coated with the oil mixture.
  5. Add Proteins & Garnish: Stir in the cooked meat, fish, or ponmo.
    Add the chopped utazi/garden egg leaves and salt to taste.
  6. Serve: Garnish with extra sliced onions and serve warm with a chilled drink.

Ofe Onugbu (Bitterleaf Soup)

Ofe Onugbu, or Bitterleaf soup, is an iconic, rich, and highly nutritious Igbo soup.
The leaves are washed thoroughly before cooking to remove bitterness.

Ingredients

  • Assorted meat (beef, goat meat, stockfish, dried fish)
  • Washed bitter leaves (onugbu)
  • Thickener (e.g., cocoyam paste, achi, or ofo)
  • Palm oil
  • Ground crayfish
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Ogiri (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Ground crayfish
  • Seasoning cubes
  • Ogiri (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Meat: Wash and season the assorted meat and stockfish with salt, seasoning cubes, and onions.
    Cook until the meat is tender. Add dry fish towards the end of the cooking process to soften.
  2. Prepare the Thickener: If using cocoyam, boil, peel, and pound into a smooth paste.
  3. Cook the Soup Base: Add red palm oil to the pot with the meat and stock. Bring to a boil, then add the
    thickener a little at a time, stirring constantly to avoid lumps, until the soup reaches the desired consistency.
  4. Seasoning: Add crayfish, ogiri, and pepper. Allow to simmer for about 10-15 minutes for the flavors to blend.
  5. Add the Leaves: Add the thoroughly washed bitter leaves to the soup.
    Stir well and let it simmer for just a few minutes, ensuring the leaves remain vibrant.
  6. Serve: Taste for salt and adjust if necessary.
    Serve hot with traditional "swallow" foods like fufu, pounded yam, or eba.

Ofe Akwu (Banga Stew/Palm Fruit Stew)

Ofe Akwu is a rich palm fruit stew typically served with white rice or yam.

Ingredients

  • 10 cups banga (palm fruit) extract, or a can of concentrated palm nut cream
  • 1 kg assorted meat (goat or beef)
  • 500g mackerel or other fish
  • 1 cup sliced scent leaves (nchanwu) or uziza leaves
  • 1/2 cup ground crayfish
  • 1 cup sliced scent leaves (nchanwu) or uziza leaves
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • Seasoning cubes, salt, and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook Meat: Season and precook the assorted meat with seasoning cubes, onions, and salt until tender.
    Remove the fish after about 10 minutes to prevent it from breaking.
  2. Extract Oil: Boil the palm fruit and then pound in a mortar. Add water and squeeze out the thick paste (extract).
    Sieve the extract into the pot with the cooked meat.
  3. Boil Stew: Bring the palm fruit extract to a boil with the meat.
    Let it simmer until the soup thickens and the oil floats to the top.
  4. Season: Add the washed dried fish, crayfish, salt, and pepper. Cook for 10 minutes.
  5. Finish: Add the precooked mackerel and sliced scent leaves. Simmer for a few more minutes.
  6. Serve: Serve the Ofe Akwu hot with boiled rice or yam.

Okpa (Bambara Nut Pudding)

Okpa is a protein-packed, soft, and flavorful pudding made from Bambara nut flour.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups okpa (Bambara nut) flour
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 3 cups warm water (adjust as needed)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers, chopped
  • 2 teaspoons ground crayfish
  • Seasoning cubes and salt to taste
  • Banana leaves, foil, or nylon wraps for steaming

Instructions

  1. Mix Dry Ingredients: In a bowl, combine okpa flour with salt, crayfish, onion, pepper, and seasoning cubes.
  2. Add Oil: Add palm oil and mix well until the flour turns a bright orange color.
  3. Create Batter: Gradually add warm water, stirring continuously to form a smooth, lump-free batter
    with a medium-thick consistency.
  4. Wrap: Scoop the batter into prepared banana leaves or wraps and tie them securely.
  5. Steam: Arrange the wrapped okpa in a pot, add water (about halfway up the wraps), cover,
    and steam on medium heat for 45 minutes to 1 hour.
  6. Serve: Unwrap and serve the Okpa warm.

Okro Soup Recipe

Fisherman okro soup is a delicious Nigerian seafood okro soup made with fresh seafood like fish, shrimp, periwinkles,
and crabs. It is light, flavorful, and best enjoyed with swallow foods like eba, fufu, or semovita.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups fresh okro, finely chopped or grated
  • 2 tablespoons palm oil
  • 500g fresh fish (catfish, tilapia, or croaker)
  • 1 cup shrimps (peeled and deveined)
  • 1 cup periwinkles (optional, washed and de-shelled)
  • 2 crabs, cleaned and cracked (optional)
  • 1/2 cup smoked or dried fish, washed and deboned
  • 2 tablespoons ground crayfish
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 cup ugu (fluted pumpkin leaves) or spinach, chopped

Instructions

  1. Prepare the seafood: Clean the fresh fish and cut it into chunks.
    Wash and prep the shrimp, crabs, and periwinkles.
  2. Cook the seafood stock: In a pot, add 3 cups of water or stock, the fresh fish, crabs, smoked fish,
    seasoning cubes, onions, and salt. Simmer for about 10 minutes until the fish is cooked.
    Carefully remove the fish to prevent breaking and set aside.
  3. Add palm oil and seasoning: Pour in the palm oil, ground crayfish, pepper, and locust beans.
    Stir and allow to cook for 3-5 minutes to release flavors.
  4. Cook the okro: Add the grated or chopped okro and stir continuously to prevent lumps.
    Let it simmer for 5 minutes on low heat.
  5. Final taste and serve: Adjust salt and seasoning as needed. Stir gently and remove from heat. Serve hot with eba, fufu, pounded yam, or semovita.
